# Features Research: Toddler Companion

## Table Stakes

### Healthy Use

- Short sessions with clear stopping points.
- Parent co-use prompts, not child-only autoplay.
- Calm pacing and low stimulation.
- No streaks, points, random rewards, infinite feeds, or pressure to continue.
- Offline or local-first progress where possible.

### Toddler Interaction

- Large tap targets.
- One clear action at a time.
- Immediate but gentle feedback.
- Real-world transfer prompts such as "Can you find something red in the room?"
- Activities that work even if the child exits early.

### Learning Content

- Everyday vocabulary.
- Colors, simple shapes, small numbers.
- Simple expressions and social language.
- Recognition from real photos.
- Repeat/respond prompts with parent support.

### Parent Experience

- Simple activity launcher.
- Brief parent guidance per activity.
- Session wind-down.
- Lightweight progress by category.
- Ability to repeat favorite items without creating addictive loops.

## Differentiators

- Real photo-first content instead of cartoon-heavy game screens.
- Parent-guided language prompts embedded in the flow.
- Anti-addiction design as a product principle.
- Screen-to-real-world handoff at the end of each short session.

## Anti-Features

- Endless levels or feeds.
- Daily streaks.
- Random rewards, loot boxes, or surprise unlocks.
- Competitive scoring.
- Fast-paced animations, noisy effects, and over-stimulating visuals.
- Autoplay sequences that keep going without parent action.
- Cloud profiles or analytics in v1.

## Research Notes

AAP guidance for ages 2-5 emphasizes high-quality content, co-viewing/co-use, and helping children apply what they learn to the world around them. WHO guidance sets a conservative ceiling for sedentary screen time for children aged 2 years. NAEYC/Fred Rogers guidance supports intentional, developmentally appropriate technology use, especially when it promotes interaction and connection rather than passive consumption.
