# Pitfalls Research: Toddler Companion

## Pitfall 1: Building a Game Loop Instead of Guided Play

**Warning signs:** Points, streaks, unlocks, celebratory loops, "next level" prompts, or unlimited retries become central to the experience.

**Prevention:** Treat each activity as a short parent-child interaction with an explicit ending. Use warm feedback, not reward mechanics.

**Phase impact:** Must be addressed in the activity engine and session design phases.

## Pitfall 2: Passive Screen Time Disguised as Learning

**Warning signs:** Autoplay, long videos, too much narration, or screens that entertain without requiring parent-child interaction.

**Prevention:** Require simple child action and parent guidance. Add screen-to-room prompts.

**Phase impact:** Content schema and activity design.

## Pitfall 3: Over-Stimulation

**Warning signs:** Fast animation, bright flashing transitions, loud sound effects, dense screens, or multiple simultaneous prompts.

**Prevention:** Use calm pacing, stable layouts, plain feedback, and one task per screen.

**Phase impact:** UI design and interaction polish.

## Pitfall 4: Progress Tracking Becomes Pressure

**Warning signs:** Percent mastery, rankings, red failure states, or parent-facing metrics that imply the child is behind.

**Prevention:** Track exposure and interests rather than performance. Use neutral language like "recently explored."

**Phase impact:** Progress store and parent dashboard.

## Pitfall 5: Child Data Privacy Creep

**Warning signs:** Accounts, cloud profiles, analytics events, voice recordings, or photo uploads appear before they are necessary.

**Prevention:** Keep v1 local-first. Avoid collecting personal child data.

**Phase impact:** Architecture and storage choices.
