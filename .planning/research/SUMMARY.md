# Research Summary: Toddler Companion

## Key Findings

**Stack:** Use a local-first mobile Web app with TypeScript, static content packs, curated real-photo assets, and local progress storage. Avoid a backend in v1 unless there is a clear need.

**Table Stakes:** Parent co-use, short sessions, calm pacing, large tap targets, real-world transfer prompts, and explicit anti-addiction constraints.

**Watch Out For:** Do not let the product drift into passive video, endless game loops, overstimulating animation, pressure-based progress metrics, or unnecessary child data collection.

## Product Implications

- The app should be designed around co-use, not babysitting.
- Session ending is a core feature, not an afterthought.
- Progress should describe exposure and interest, not achievement pressure.
- Real photos and screen-to-room prompts support transfer from digital recognition to everyday life.
- v1 can be useful without accounts, cloud sync, or a large content library.

## Requirements Implications

The first release should include:

- A mobile-first activity shell.
- A small real-photo content pack.
- Picture selection, find-it, and repeat/respond activity types.
- A session timer with gentle wind-down.
- Anti-addiction interaction rules.
- Local simple progress tracking.
- A parent-facing dashboard.
